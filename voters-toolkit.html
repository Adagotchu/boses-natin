Step 2

The Platform Auditor

Don't just listen to the jingles. Audit the promises using logic.

1. Legal Power: Can they actually do it?

Verify if the promise matches their office. A Mayor fixes local roads; a Senator writes national laws. If a local candidate promises to change the national constitution, they are overpromising.

2. S.M.A.R.T: Is it a plan or a dream?

Is it Specific? Instead of "Improve Education," look for "Build 500 libraries." A real plan has numbers and a timeline. No numbers = No accountability.

3. Consistency: Who is supporting them?

Check if their stand on issues changes depending on who they are talking to. Also, look at their campaign donors—does the candidate represent the people, or just the big businesses that funded their ads?

Step 3

Finding the "Resibo"

Don't rely on TikTok or rumors. Use these official government portals to see the actual performance and records of our officials.

Election Day "Baon"

No Selfies with Ballot

It is illegal to take a photo of your filled-out ballot. Your vote is secret!

Bring a Kodigo

Write your chosen candidates on a piece of paper. You can't use your phone inside the precinct.

Shade Properly

Fully shade the circle. Don't check or cross it out, or the machine won't count it.
