Fact-Check Guide

In a world of "fake news," the best defense is a sharp mind. Learn how to spot the truth.

How to Spot a "Red Flag" Post

Emotional Bait

Is the post trying to make you angry, afraid, or overly excited? Lies often hide behind intense emotions.

The "Source: Trust Me"

No link? No name? If it just says "From a friend" or "Forwarded many times," be suspicious.

Recycled News

Check the date. Old photos or news are often re-shared as if they happened today to fit a narrative.

Use the S.I.F.T. Method

S

STOP

Take a breath. If a post makes you angry or proud, that’s a signal to check it before sharing.

I

INVESTIGATE

Check the source. Is the website well-known? Does the author have a track record of truth?

F

FIND

Look for trusted coverage. See if established news outlets like GMA or ABS-CBN are reporting the same thing.

T

TRACE

Trace the claims to the original context. Was that video or photo actually from 5 years ago?
